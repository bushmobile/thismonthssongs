This Month's Songs

Jun 2020

It's been a while since the previous list of songs! Here are ten more for a listen.

I'm starting with Kacey Musgraves's Late To The Party. Somehow a country music outsider and its biggest star, this song inspired me to check out the rest of her not-country-but-country work • I can't help but bounce along to the beat of Fancy Man by Devendra Banhart - surely, we all like to think of ourselves as a Fancy Man sometimes • Although by Carlos Forster, the next song, Campfire Songs is actually a duet with M Ward - hence the reason I came across it • In This Tornado Love You, smokey voiced Neko Case sings about a tornado that's in love with someone - yes, it's that literal - apparently it was a dream she had • I'd vaguely heard of Vampire Weekend, but hadn't ever heard their songs. My favourite line from Hannah Hunt is A man of faith said hidden eyes could see what I was thinking / I just smiled and told him that was only true of Hannah - I still haven't heard any of their other songs • The three voices in Alialujah Choir come together to create the beautifully haunting harmonies of A House, A Home • A (relatively) stripped back St. Vincent sings a brief semi-affectionate ode in New York (if you can affectionately call someone a motherfucker) • Everytime I hear Phosporescent's infectiously catchy New Birth In New England I can't help but crank up the volume and sing along • Somehow, I made it passed my mid-30s before I actually listened to the album Grace by Jeff Buckley. Obviously, I knew who he was (Hallelujah, durr), but perhaps I innately knew I wouldn't be emotionally ready until then. Goodness, almost any track on that album could have made it into this slot, but the raw feeling and soaring vocals of Lover, You Should Have Come Over make it my top choice. I'm listening to it now - I change my mind, I'm not emotionally ready • I originally had All Your Favourite Bands by Dawes before JB on the playlist, but I realised I had to end it on a positive note and swapped the songs. All Your Favourite Bands isn't the world's most complex or musically challenging song, but what it is, is a near perfect sing-a-long anthem.

So these are the ten songs this month - expect more soon, but in the meantime, may all your favourite bands stay together.
